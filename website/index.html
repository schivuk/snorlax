18-549 Team 1: Snorlax

A smart bed.

Team Members: Ameya Kamat, Benson Qiu, Harsha Chivukula, Pratik Prakash

Concept

Snorlax is a smart mattress pad aimed at allowing people to measure their sleeping patterns. The mattress pad consists of a network of pressure sensors and accelerometers inside the mattress to measure the position and movements of the user, calculate the user's sleeping patterns and communicate this information to a web application, that end-users can use to control the system.

Motivation

Currently, sleeping patterns can be monitored by the average user through various phone apps and wearable devices such as Fitbit, which are all intrusive and use primarily the user's movement to calculate sleeping patterns. The incentive for this project was to have an entirely non-intrusive system (including wearables), that augments the functionality of current mobile phone applications. By spanning the entire mattress with sensor coverage and having multiple accelerometers at different parts of the mattress, our goal is to broaden the sensor data that phones can collect and have more accurate sleep information.

Competitive Analysis

  • Eight Sleep: This is our primary competitor, and is a smart mattress pad that does all of the sleep tracking without the need of a wearable device. It has features to heat the mattress pad to desired temperature, communicate with Nest thermostat, control lighting and monitor heart rate and noise levels. This product is still in development and it is unclear if it will have all the features suggested.
  • Sleep as Android: A sleep tracking Android app that determines the user’s optimal waking point through the use of accelerometers. The main differentiation of our product from this app is that the app requires the phone to be put on the bed in order to get any readings, while our product does not require any conscious effort to do anything different to your normal sleep routine.
  • RestBed: A smart, actuated bed that tracks sleep data and responds to your sleep. While RestBed provides more comprehensive data on the way someone sleeps, it is very expensive, costing over $200. Our product tracks users' movements, sleeping positions and any noise to determine the sleep data at a smaller cost.

Design Requirements

  • General: The system shall consist of a network of sensors that are embedded into a mattress pad, so the product can be used by placing it on top of any mattress. The product shall be used to track body positions/movements during sleep, to control an alarm, and to allow users to view sleep data over the internet.
  • Hardware components: The main hardware components shall involve a network of pressure sensors, at least 2 accelerometers, a microphone, an MCU to measure sensor data, a WiFi module to transmit the data to a web server, an amplifier-speaker system to sound alarms, and a computer to store and analyse the data.
  • Non-invasiveness: The Snorlax mattress pad shall be entirely non-invasive by having a design such that the sensor network shall be located on the bottom of the mattress pad, conductive thread shall be used for wiring the sensor network and the user will have no wearable component.
  • Application interfacing: The user shall be able to register their smart mattress pad to their preferred WiFi network using their phone (by entering in the network name and password). The mattress pad will then connect to the specified WiFi network and communicate / log data to the web server over the internet.
  • Authentication: The web server should check for authentication when receiving data from the smart mattress pad, to ensure that arbitrary external requests do not modify logging data.
  • Memory footprint: Logging sensor data at very frequent intervals can lead to an arbitrarily large memory load for the server. Measures must be taken to log only important information such as position changes, reading spikes from the accelerometer or microphone.
  • Alarm: The smart mattress pad shall have an alarm feature, whereby users can set an alarm time through the web application. The smart mattress pad shall find an optimal time for the user to wake up based on estimated sleep cycle detection, and sound the alarm at the optimal time within a 30 minute window of the specified time. The smart mattress pad shall detect if the user is still on the bed, and only turn off the alarm after the user gets off the bed.
  • User interface: Users will have access to a web application that they can log into and view reports of their sleep activity over the night. Users will be able to setup an account and link their mattress pad with the account. The user’s data will be sourced from the mattress pad that had been registered with their account. Users will be able to view a graph showing them occurences of the following events:
    • Getting out of the bed sometime at night
    • Switching from lying on back to stomach
    • Change in body position
    • Still periods with little or no movement
  • Performance & Comfort: A poll/survey on the users of the product should yield that at least 95% users do not feel anything out of the ordinary when sleeping on the mattress pad.
